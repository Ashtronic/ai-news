OpenAI is collapsing the distance between model and platform — Codex is now a product suite, not a feature

Today's releases show a clear strategic pattern: OpenAI shipped GPT-5.5 (smarter model), Codex Automations (autonomous scheduling), Plugins/Skills (tool integration), and workspace structure all in the same news cycle. This is not coincidental. OpenAI is positioning Codex as a full-stack agent development environment — model, orchestration, scheduling, and tooling in one surface. For builders, the risk is building custom orchestration layers that Codex is about to commoditize. The opportunity is betting on this platform early and reducing your own infrastructure footprint. Google's TPU v8 announcement on the same day underscores that the compute layer is also racing to match agentic demand — the infrastructure and model layers are co-evolving faster than most product roadmaps can track.
