OpenAI is collapsing the agentic stack into a single product surface

Today's Codex relaunch — combining scheduling, triggers, plugins, skills, workspaces, and a smarter underlying model in GPT-5.5 — is a deliberate move to own the full agentic workflow layer, not just the model. Teams currently stitching together LangChain, cron jobs, Zapier, and GPT-4o API calls should pressure-test whether Codex now covers 80% of their orchestration needs out of the box. The risk of building on this stack is vendor lock-in; the reward is shipping faster. The builders who win in the next 6 months will be those who correctly identify which parts of their agentic infrastructure are commodity versus differentiated — and ruthlessly outsource the former.
